Research

Summary

My research focusses on the ballot initiative process in the United States, and how actors across the federal system interact to disrupt the status quo. I am very interested in how actors leverage the federal structure for policymaking purposes, and the nature of the relationships between levels of government.

My latest project is looking at campaign finance contributions on a comparative scale within US ballot initiative races - what type of groups finance campaigns, how much does each group contribute, and are some forms of campaign contributions more effective than others?

Previously, I worked on drug liberalisation policies passed via the initiative process. With funding from the Rothermere American Institute, I was able to conduct fieldwork in Oregon, interviewing ballot initiative proponents and state politicians surrounding the two most recent marijuana legalisation ballot measures (Measures 80 and 91).
